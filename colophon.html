How this was built

This page documents the research, tools, and thinking behind a personalized conversation canvas built for a coffee chat with Henrik Berggren, Head of Product & Design at Mutiny.

The concept

A reverse Mutiny

Mutiny builds 1:1 personalized microsites to help companies close enterprise deals. The core product uses AI to research a target account, personalize content to their needs, and deliver it as a tailored web experience.

This microsite applies the same logic in reverse: I'm the seller, Henrik and Mutiny are the account, and the "AI research" was real research into who Henrik is, what he's built, what he values, and what he's looking for in a Senior Product Designer.

It was designed as a live screen-share companion during a 30-minute conversation — not a presentation, but an open notebook on the table between two people.

Process

How it came together

  • Research phase
    Three parallel research agents analyzed Henrik's background, Mutiny's product, and the interview process — one writing a conversation script, one simulating Henrik's perspective, one providing strategic analysis from a Head of Product lens.
  • Concept
    The "reverse Mutiny" idea emerged from the meta-connection: demonstrating understanding of the product by using its own methodology. The UX shifted from a traditional microsite to a "conversation canvas" once the decision was made to screen-share live.
  • Content architecture
    Every section maps to a specific signal Henrik scans for in candidates (sourced from research). The structure follows the natural flow of a hiring conversation, not a portfolio structure.
  • Design + build
    Single HTML file, no frameworks. Typography and spacing chosen to echo the minimal, craft-first aesthetic Henrik values (informed by Readmill's design philosophy). Built to be scrolled casually, not clicked through.
  • Personalization
    Content was personalized using Bobby Kenner's actual project history and design philosophy, mapped against Mutiny's stated needs and Henrik's known evaluation criteria.
