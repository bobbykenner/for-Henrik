An initial point of view

How I'd think about the Creative Agent

The core tension I see: the Creative Agent needs to feel fast enough to be worth using and trustworthy enough that a marketer at Qualtrics would put their brand behind it. Those pull in opposite directions.

My instinct is that the answer isn't a binary "approve/reject" gate. It's designing a collaboration loop — where the AI generates, the human steers, and each cycle makes the output more aligned. The interface shouldn't ask "is this right?" It should ask "which direction?"

The biggest design risk is building something that feels like a chatbot with a publish button. The opportunity is making it feel like a creative partner that already knows your brand, your audience, and your intent.

This is a starting intuition, not a proposal. I'd want to talk to your customers and see the current experience before going deeper — but I wanted you to know I've been thinking about it.
